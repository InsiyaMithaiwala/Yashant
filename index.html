Reasons why people living in poverty may not have access to Education

Lack of financial resources

Many families living in poverty cannot afford to pay for education, whether it be the cost of tuition, books, uniforms, transportation, or other expenses

Lack of infrastructure

In some areas, there may not be enough schools or teachers to provide education to all children, particularly in rural or remote areas

Discrimination

Children from marginalized groups, such as ethnic or religious minorities, may face discrimination or prejudice that prevents them from accessing education

Poor health and nutrition

Malnutrition and poor health can affect a child's ability to learn and attend school regularly

Cultural attitudes and practices

Some cultures may not value education, particularly for girls or certain ethnic or social groups

Distance and transportation

Even if there is a school nearby, poor families may not have reliable transportation to get their children to school, especially in rural or isolated areas
